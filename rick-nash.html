Career

During his high school years, Nash started his ice hockey career as a junior in the Greater Toronto Hockey League (GTHL). In 1999, he drew the attention of OHL scouts while playing for GTHL. Following that, he was signed by the London Knights for two seasons in the Ontario Hockey League. While playing with London Knight, he got the Emms Family Award as the League’s outstanding rookie. The Columbus Blue Jackets picked him first overall in the 2002 NHL Entry Draft in the 2002-03 season.

On October 10, 2002, he made his NHL debut, scoring a goal in a 2-1 victory against the Chicago Blackhawks. He was nominated for the Calder Trophy as the NHL’s outstanding rookie that season. Nash played with HC Davos of the Swiss Nationalliga A (NLA) during the 2004-05 NHL lockout. During the 2005-06 NHL season, he joined the Columbus Blue Jackets. He was a member of the club until the 2011-12 season. During the 2012-13 season, he was signed by the New York Rangers. He has since joined the Rangers. Rick has played for the Canadian national team on multiple occasions. In 2010 and 2014, he won gold and two silver medals at the World Championships, as well as two gold medals at the Winter Olympics.
